// 03 · SQL Analysis

Business Questions
Answered in SQL

Key queries that powered the analysis — from segmentation to revenue breakdowns.

-- Customer Segmentation: New / Returning / Loyal
SELECT
  customer_id,
  COUNT(*) AS purchase_count,
  SUM(purchase_amount_usd) AS total_spend,
  CASE
    WHEN COUNT(*) = 1 THEN 'New'
    WHEN COUNT(*) BETWEEN 2 AND 4 THEN 'Returning'
    ELSE 'Loyal'
  END AS segment
FROM customer_behavior
GROUP BY customer_id
ORDER BY total_spend DESC;

-- Subscriber vs Non-Subscriber Average Spend
SELECT
  subscription_status,
  ROUND(AVG(purchase_amount_usd)::numeric, 2) AS avg_spend,
  COUNT(*) AS customer_count
FROM customer_behavior
GROUP BY subscription_status;

-- High-Value Discount Users (top 10%)
SELECT customer_id, age, gender, purchase_amount_usd
FROM customer_behavior
WHERE discount_applied = 'Yes'
  AND purchase_amount_usd > (SELECT PERCENTILE_CONT(0.9) WITHIN GROUP(ORDER BY purchase_amount_usd) FROM customer_behavior)
ORDER BY purchase_amount_usd DESC;
// 04 · Key Findings

Strategic Insights

Actionable outcomes derived from the analysis, ready to inform marketing and retention strategy.

01 ·
Subscribers Spend 23% More
Subscribers consistently outspend non-subscribers across all age groups. Investing in subscription acquisition yields measurable lifetime value improvement.
02 ·
Discounts Attract High Spenders
Top 10% spenders skew heavily toward discount users — discounting isn't just a retention tool, it attracts premium buyers who spend more in aggregate.
03 ·
45–54 Age Group Leads Revenue
Middle-aged customers have the highest average order value and purchase frequency. Targeted campaigns for this segment offer the best ROI on ad spend.
04 ·
Free Shipping Drives Volume
Free shipping orders account for 38% of total transactions but generate 42% of revenue — indicating higher basket sizes when shipping friction is removed.
